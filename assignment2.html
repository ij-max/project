Exotic dance moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances. To answer your question, my favourite dances are as follows, with definitions:

Polynesian chicken dance

A little known but very influential dance dating back as far as 300BC, a whole village would dance around in a circle like chickens, to encourage their livestock to be "fruitful"
Iceland brownian shuffle
Before the Icelanders developed fire as a means of getting warm, they used to pratice this dance, which involved huddling close together in a circle on the floor, and shuffling their bodies around in imperceptibly tiny, very rapid movements. One of my fellow students used to say that he thought this dance was inspired modern styles such as twerking
Artic robot dance
An interesting example of historic misinformation, English explorers in the 1960s believed to have discovered a new dance style characterised by "robotic", stilted movements, being praticed by inhabitants of Northern Alaska and Canada. Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this they were really cold.

For more of my research, see my exotic dance research page.
